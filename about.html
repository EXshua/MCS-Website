About MCS

Built for operators, not administrators

Small vessel operators are experts at running their vessels. We're experts at keeping the compliance side of the job under control. That's the partnership.

What We Do

We carry the compliance burden so you don't have to

Running a commercial vessel under 24m means being responsible for a constant, rolling calendar of safety equipment services, certificate renewals, and regulatory requirements. It's not a one-off job — it's ongoing, and the consequences of getting it wrong range from a failed inspection to a grounded vessel.

Marine Compliance Solutions was built specifically to take that burden off small operators who can't justify a full-time compliance resource but still need the job done properly.

We maintain a live register of every compliance item on your vessel, issue structured alerts ahead of every expiry, and — depending on your service tier — coordinate the action needed to keep your vessel in good standing. Every month, you receive a plain-English summary of your current compliance position.

Our Role

What we do — and what we don't

MCS is a compliance management and coordination partner. We're rigorous, we're proactive, and we make sure the right things happen at the right time. But we are not a regulatory body and we do not hold statutory authority.

Statutory responsibility for vessel compliance — including safety equipment, certifications, and regulatory adherence — remains with the Vessel Owner and Master. MCS supports and manages the compliance process; we do not replace or absorb those legal obligations.

Advisory Notice: MCS provides compliance management and coordination services. Ultimate statutory responsibility remains with the Vessel Owner / Master at all times.

In scope vs. out of scope

  • ✓ Tracking and alerting on compliance expiries
  • ✓ Coordinating suppliers and booking services
  • ✓ Managing documentation and compliance records
  • ✓ Survey and inspection preparation support
  • ✓ Monthly compliance reporting and status updates
  • ✓ Proactive planning and forward scheduling
  • ✗ Assuming statutory responsibility for vessel compliance
  • ✗ Acting as a regulatory authority or classification society
  • ✗ Conducting physical inspections or certifying equipment
  • ✗ Replacing the obligations of the Vessel Owner or Master
